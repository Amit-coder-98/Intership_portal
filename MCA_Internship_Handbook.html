Roles & responsibilities

Student

  • Register internship & submit offer letter, Proposal and undertaking before starting.
  • Maintain a daily logbook and submit a weekly progress report every week (online).
  • Attend scheduled faculty review meetings and final viva.
  • Maintain professional conduct; follow company/mentor rules.
  • Submit final report, completion certificate, and slides by deadline.

Industry Mentor (Company Representative)

  • Provide induction, assign tasks & working hours, and supervise the student.
  • Complete Mid-Term Evaluation (20 marks) and Final Evaluation (30 marks) using the provided 2D rubrics.
  • Verify attendance and issue completion certificate on company letterhead.
  • Give constructive feedback and sign the required forms.

Internship Coordinator (Program Head / Faculty)

  • Approve internship registrations and maintain the master tracking sheet.
  • Organize and lead fortnightly review meetings; record minutes/marks.
  • Review weekly reports and final reports; assign coordinator marks (50).
  • Consolidate industry and coordinator marks, compute final marks and map to credits.
  • Communicate deadlines and serve as escalation contact.

Registration & mandatory documents (online)

Before internship starts (by 15 Jan):

  • Signed Offer Letter (PDF) uploaded to student folder.
  • Internship proposal
  • Undertaking

During internship:

  • Daily logbook
  • Weekly Progress Reports (each week) — standard template.
  • Faculty review meeting notes (fortnightly).

Mid-term:

  • Mentor Mid-Term Evaluation Form

At the end:

  • Mentor Final Evaluation Form
  • Internship Completion Certificate (company letterhead — period, role, hours).
  • Final Internship Report (Word/PDF as per template).
  • Presentation slides for viva.

Attendance, conduct & eligibility rules

  • Minimum eligibility: Student must complete ≥ 85% of agreed internship days/hours. Failure → may lead to “Incomplete” regardless of marks.
  • Attendance is verified by the industry mentor and cross-checked by coordinator.
  • Professional conduct required; serious misconduct reported by mentor may lead to termination of internship credit.
  • Plagiarism: Final report must be original. Any detected plagiarism will be handled per university policy (penalty up to disqualification).

Assessment structure — marks → credits

Total: 100 marks = 8 credits

Industry Mentor — 50 marks

  • Final Evaluation: 50 marks (end of internship)

Internship Coordinator / Faculty — 50 marks

  • Weekly Progress Reports: 20 marks
  • Faculty Review Meetings (Fortnightly): 10 marks
  • Final Internship Report: 10 marks
  • Presentation & Viva: 10 marks

Credit mapping (final)

  • 90–100 → 8 credits
  • 80–89 → 7 credits
  • 70–79 → 6 credits
  • 60–69 → 5 credits
  • 50–59 → 4 credits
  • < 50 → Incomplete / Re-internship required

Note: Attendance < 75% makes a student ineligible for credit even if marks otherwise qualify.

Submission rules & file naming convention

  • All documents uploaded to the assigned Microsoft/Google Drive folder by deadlines.
  • File naming: RollNo_LastName_DocType_Internship
    • Ex: MCA401_Agarwal_OfferLetter_Internship.pdf
    • Weekly report: MCA401_Agarwal_Week05_Report.pdf
  • Mentor evaluations must be on company letterhead or signed PDF.
  • Late submissions accepted only with documented permission (coordinator discretion).
